More than 40% of mammalian genome are transposable elements, which are thought as "dark matters" in genome. Their ancestor elements could jump around in genome, but now they are silenced by host cells at most of the time to protect cells from genome instability. Sometimes transposable elements are expressed and functional.
Therefore, we asked:
1. What's the role of transposable elements in stem cells?
2. How are transposable elements regulated during development?
